Glocholash Grammar

Nouns

Participant roles

The nominative or subject form is simply the dictionary form.

The object is marked by word order.

The genitive or posessive is marked by vowel raising.

Prepositions are formed by a series of suffixes.

Number

Number is expressed in various ways:

1: Infixing

For older words, an infixing syllable is used.

Number Infix Example Meaning
1 0 kesuk one hill
2 (e)ra kraesuk two hills
3 (e)ro kroesuk three hills
4 (e)la klaesuk four hills

The fourth is archaic, as is the third in some dialects.

2: Reduplication

To express generic plurality (ie two or more), the first syllable can reduplicate. Eg: carid 'carriage' cacaraid 'carriages' This is the productive plural, and used with nearly all loan words and modern words.

2: Suppletion

A few nouns have suppletive plurals:
singular dual plural meaning
glov s̊erodar s̊erodar man
byor heraimamus heroimamus woman
fast year

Animacy

Nouns are not differentiated by gender but by animacy. The following types of nouns are, as a rule, considered animate: humans, animals, heavenly bodies, weather, machines, trees, plants, flowing water, musical instruments Animacy is marked by the final consonant being geminate. Thus glov 'man' [glov:], birid 'bird' [birid:]. Animacy is also marked by the article suffix.
