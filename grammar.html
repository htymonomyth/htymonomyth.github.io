At start of a word, fricatives must be voiced, but can be spelled with unvoiced consonant.
At the end of a word, fricatives must be unvoiced.
Cannot have a nasal + stop, the nasal vanishes.
Cannot start a word with [n]
In classical Glocholash, there are no dipthongs or glides.

Word Order

Glocholash has strict word order. It is VSO (VSaOa).

Morphology

These are the following ways that words change in Glocholash.

Reduplication

The first syllable is doubled. Any clusters are simplified and the vowel is unstressed, usually written as an i or the same as the original consonant. Thus dras̊el > didras̊el.

Infixing

The initial syllable is infixed with another syllable:kesuk one hill: ra + kraesuk (k-ra-esuk) 'two hills'

Vowel raising and lowering

The vowel in the first syllable is raised:
a > e > i > i
y > o > u > u

Affixing

The adding of some prefixes and/or suffixes.

Nouns

Participant roles

The nominative or subject form is simply the dictionary form.

The object is marked by word order.

The genitive or posessive is marked by vowel raising.

Prepositions are formed by a series of suffixes.

Number

Number is expressed in various ways:

1: Infixing

For older words, an infixing syllable is used.

Number Infix Example Meaning
1 0 kesuk one hill
2 (e)ra kraesuk two hills
3 (e)ro kroesuk three hills
4 (e)la klaesuk four hills

The fourth is archaic, as is the third in some dialects.

2: Reduplication

To express generic plurality (ie two or more), the first syllable can reduplicate. Eg: carid 'carriage' cacaraid 'carriages' This is the productive plural, and used with nearly all loan words and modern words.

2: Suppletion

A few nouns have suppletive plurals:
singular dual plural meaning
glov s̊erodar s̊erodar man
byor heraimamus heroimamus woman
fast year

Animacy

Nouns are not differentiated by gender but by animacy. The following types of nouns are, as a rule, considered animate:

humans, animals, heavenly bodies, weather, machines, trees, plants, flowing water, musical instruments

Animacy is marked by the final consonant being geminate. Thus glov 'man' [glov:], birid 'bird' [birid:].

Animacy is also marked by the article suffix:

Meaning Animate Non-Animate
The -en -ek
Collective -(r)uk -(r)in

The article is used in a sense of 'aforementioned', it is not used as much as in English.
The collective marker uses r in nouns ending in a vowel. It is used always in the singular to denote 'group of', 'flock of'.

Agent noun

For adjectives: Animate: is(e)- / Inanimate: in(e)- as a prefix. 'One or thing that is'

skevis 'drunk' > iskevis 'drunkard' (*ise-skevis)
mnak 'big' > inemnak 'a big thing, mass, lump'

For nouns:
tu- + VR = -er. eg. cutter, dlen 'cut', tudlin = 'cutter' [tudlin:] an., 'someone who cuts' but [tudlin] in. 'thing that cuts, eg knife, scissors etc'

Compounds

Endocentric Compounds: Noun + Noun eg kennel, doghouse (lit. 'house of dogs') can 'house' dok, gen. pl. dra.uk = candrauk

Exocentric Compounds: Noun + adj (noun in genitive) eg. (one who is) barefoot. (lit. 'foot of bareness') n̊apre 'bare'+ wid̊i 'foot' = wid̊in̥epre

Copulative Compounds: Noun + Noun + -don eg parents (lit. 'mother and father') lata 'mother', ks̊ut father = lataks̊uton (from lataks̊utdon)

Prepositions

Prepositions are suffixes. They are used either with the headform to denote a stationary situation, or with VR to denote movement and direction.


Suffix Stationary Movement Examples
-ok at, in(side) into canok 'in the house' / cenok 'into the house'
-in on onto canon 'on the house' / cenon 'onto the house'
-or under under canor 'under the house' / cenor 'under the house' (from elsewhere)
-an over onto canan 'over the house' / cenan 'down onto the house'
-astar by / next to towards canastar 'by house' / cenastar 'towards (but not into) house'

These suffixes go after any definite suffix, thus canekok 'in the aforementioned house'

Adjectives

Adjectives operate as nouns in the genitive after a noun. Thus 'red river' can be understood as 'river of redness': s̊ebon 'river'+ dras̊o 'red' = s̊ebondres̊o 'red river', but tre s̊ebon dras̊o 'the river is red'.

superlative

To say something is superlative or is 'very'use reduplication, thus didras̊o 'very red' or 'reddest'

comparative

To say something is more, infix -le-, thus drales̊o 'redder''.

'as X as'

Infix -(r)- mnak 'big' as big as mnerak. 'I am as big as him' tre fi mnerak ya.

Adverbs

Adverbs work as nouns in the genitive after the verb.
